About the Workshop

Connect Communities

Create a platform for connecting various communities, fostering collaboration for the advancement of a compositional theory of decision making.

All Experience Levels

Open to participants from diverse backgrounds and experience levels, catering to both newcomers and seasoned professionals.

Practical Insights

Gain valuable insights into addressing intricate decision-making problems through success stories and practical tools from applied category theory.

Distinguished experts in the field will deliver talks, showcasing the practical implementation of these tools to tackle real-world issues. These presentations will also offer valuable perspectives on potential directions for future research endeavors, and for creating a new, interdisciplinary community around these topics.

Workshop Schedule

1:45pm - 2:00pm 15 min

Workshop Opening & Motivation

All Organizers

Introduction to the workshop and motivation.

Abstract

Welcome to our workshop on Applied Category Theory for Compositional Decision Making. We'll introduce the motivation behind bringing together diverse communities to advance compositional approaches to complex decision-making problems. This opening will set the stage for the day's presentations and establish the interdisciplinary nature of our discussions.

2:00pm - 3:00pm 60 min

Primer on Applied Category Theory

Zardini Fairbanks Ames

Foundational concepts with examples from various disciplines.

Abstract

This comprehensive introduction covers the fundamental concepts of applied category theory, illustrated through concrete examples from control theory, robotics, and computational science. We'll explore how categorical thinking provides a unified language for compositional design and decision-making across diverse domains. Participants will gain the mathematical foundations needed to understand the subsequent technical presentations.

3:00pm - 3:40pm 40 min

Coffee Break & Networking

Open Discussion

Networking opportunity and informal discussions.

About

Take this opportunity to connect with fellow attendees, discuss the morning's presentations, and network with researchers from different fields. Coffee and light refreshments will be provided. This is an excellent time to establish collaborations and exchange ideas about compositional approaches to decision-making.

3:40pm - 4:05pm 25 min

Introduction to Categorical Lyapunov Theory

Ames Moeller

Categorical foundations for stability analysis.

Abstract

We present a categorical approach to Lyapunov theory that provides a compositional framework for stability analysis of complex dynamical systems. This work extends classical Lyapunov methods using category theory to enable modular reasoning about system stability. We'll demonstrate how this framework allows for hierarchical decomposition of stability proofs and compositional design of stable control systems.

4:05pm - 4:30pm 25 min

Scientific Computing with Categories

Fairbanks Hanks

Categorical approaches to computational methods.

Abstract

This talk explores how category theory provides powerful abstractions for scientific computing, enabling compositional design of numerical algorithms and optimization methods. We'll discuss applications to model predictive control, showing how categorical frameworks can make complex computational pipelines more modular, reusable, and mathematically principled. Examples will include implementations using modern scientific computing tools.

4:30pm - 4:55pm 25 min

Compositional Design of Complex Systems

Zardini

System-level design using compositional principles.

Abstract

We present a compositional approach to designing complex autonomous systems, from hardware selection to high-level decision making. This talk demonstrates how category theory provides a unified framework for reasoning about multi-scale system design, enabling principled composition of subsystems while maintaining global properties. Case studies from autonomous driving and robotics will illustrate the practical benefits of this approach.

4:55pm - 5:20pm 25 min

Compositional Modeling of Sequential Decision Systems

Hale

Category theory for decision-making processes.

Abstract

This presentation introduces categorical methods for modeling and analyzing sequential decision-making systems. We explore how category theory provides natural abstractions for temporal reasoning and compositional construction of decision policies. The talk will cover applications to reinforcement learning, planning, and multi-agent coordination, demonstrating how categorical thinking enables more modular and interpretable decision-making algorithms.

5:20pm - 5:30pm 10 min

Closing Remarks & Future Directions

All Organizers

Wrap-up and discussion of future research directions.

Summary

We'll conclude the workshop with a summary of key insights and discussion of future research directions in applied category theory for decision making. This session will highlight opportunities for collaboration and outline potential next steps for building an interdisciplinary community around these topics.
